Security Foundations: Governance & Frameworks
The CIA Triad
The CIA Triad serves as a core model designed to guide policies for information security within an organization. Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessed only by authorized users, while Integrity focuses on protecting data from unauthorized modification to maintain its accuracy. Availability completes the triad by ensuring that systems and data are consistently accessible to authorized users whenever they are needed.

The AAA Framework
The AAA Framework provides a structured approach to managing access control and accountability. Authentication is the process of verifying a user's identity, often through passwords or tokens, whereas Authorization determines the specific permissions and access levels granted to that user. Finally, Accounting involves tracking and recording user activity and resource consumption, which is essential for forensic auditing and compliance reporting.

Governance and Cybersecurity Roles
Governance provides the strategic roadmap that defines how an organization protects its assets, ensuring that security measures align with business goals and legal requirements. It affects cybersecurity roles by establishing the policies, standards, and procedures that practitioners must follow, transforming technical tasks into repeatable, legally defensible processes. Without governance, technical roles operate in a vacuum without clear accountability or risk management priorities.
